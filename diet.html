🍱 Fixed Diet Chart

Same menu every day = no decisions, no leaks, gentle deficit. Built on your foods.
The deal: eat the portions below, skip random extras, 1 sweet/day. That alone creates the gentle deficit (~0.3–0.5 kg/week). We don't starve — you're training for a half marathon; the food fuels the work. Portions are the instruction; the kcal notes are just for awareness.
For run/gym days — a bit more fuel around workouts.
🌅 6:00 AM Wake / pre-workout
~90
  • 1–2 glasses warm water (start your 3L)
  • 4–5 almonds + 2 walnuts (soaked overnight)
  • → then your run or gym session
💪 Post-workout within 30 min
~100
  • 1 banana OR 1 glass milk — refuels muscle + kills the sweet craving
  • Amla juice (optional, as you like)
🍳 9:00 AM Breakfast
~580
  • 3 boiled eggs (whole)
  • 1 millet main — pick one: 2 ragi/kambu dosa · OR 1 bowl kambu/ragi koozh · OR 1 bowl barnyard pongal
  • 1 small cup sundal (chickpea / moong / peanut)
  • Coffee/tea — no sugar (½ tsp max)
🥛 1:00 PM Lunch
~600
  • 1 level cup rice (not heaped) OR 2 millet rotis
  • 1 bowl dal / sambar
  • 1 cup curd
  • 1 cup vegetable (beetroot/carrot/beans, cluster beans, ivy gourd, raw banana…)
  • Keerai (greens) when available · paneer 100g on 3 days/week
🥤 4:00 PM Afternoon fuel
~140
  • 1 glass buttermilk (moru)
  • Small handful roasted chana / peanuts — your default snack, reach for THIS not sweets
🌙 7:00 PM Dinner (lighter, earlier)
~420
  • 2 boiled eggs OR egg curry
  • 1 vegetable + small carb (2 dosa, or 1 cup veg + curd)
  • 1 fruit
Training day ≈ ~1950–2050 kcal + 1 sweet · protein ~85–90g · gentle deficit ✅

The 6 rules that make it work

  • Portions as written — a "cup" is level, not heaped. The portion IS the deficit.
  • 1 sweet/day max — post-workout, on a plate, rest kept out of sight (Second-Sweet Rule).
  • No random extras between meals — if hungry, reach for the chana/buttermilk/fruit, never the snack box.
  • 3L water/day — thirst fakes hunger; hydration cuts false cravings + helps runs in the heat.
  • Protein every meal — your eggs/dal/curd/sundal already cover it. No counting needed.
  • Eat MORE on hard training days if genuinely hungry — extra rice/dosa/fruit, never sweets. Fuel the work.
