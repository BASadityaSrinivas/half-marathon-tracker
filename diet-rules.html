📖 The rules & why

Read this once. Come back when a question comes up. The menu lives on the daily page — this page is only the thinking behind it.
The deal: eat the portions on the chart, no random extras, 1 sweet a day. That's a gentle deficit (~0.3–0.5 kg/wk) — we don't starve. You're building for a half-marathon; the food has to fuel the work. A chart that's too lean doesn't make you leaner, it makes you binge.

✋ How to measure — no scale needed

You overeat by habit, not hunger — so control volume with your hand and one fixed bowl. Precision isn't the point; consistency is.
  • Carbs (rice) = 1 fist / 1 level katori
  • Protein (eggs/dal/paneer) = 1 palm
  • Veg = 2 fists (eat freely)
  • Snacks (chana/nuts) = 1 cupped hand
  • Dry fruits/fats = 1 thumb
🥔 Potato counts as RICE, not vegetable. On the potato day there is no rice — never both. Boiled or light poriyal only; urulai roast/varuval ≈ 250–350 kcal (potato is a sponge for oil). A real veg still comes alongside it.

🍽️ Beat overeating (matters more than any number)

This is your actual problem — not what you eat, how much. Fix the environment; don't rely on willpower.
  • Serve once, put the pot away — plate it in the kitchen, then sit. No refills within reach. (Biggest lever on this page.)
  • Smaller plate — your brain reads a full plate as a full meal.
  • Water before every meal, and eat slow — wait 15 min before seconds (fullness lags ~20 min).
  • Never eat straight from the container.

🆓 Free list — eat/drink ANYTIME you're hungry

No cap, no fixed slot, no counting. These are volume without calories; for you that's a weapon, not a loophole.
  • Raw carrot (1 medium)~25 kcal
  • Cucumber / raw tomato~20 kcal
  • Raw amla — 1–2 a day~10 kcal
  • Hibiscus tea / infusion — no sugar~5 kcal
  • Drumstick (murungai) soup~40–60 kcal
  • Buttermilk (moru)~40 kcal
  • Plain / warm water, black coffee or tea — no sugar~0 kcal
Two ways to actually use these:
  • Before lunch/dinner — arrive at the meal already part-full, so the meal shrinks itself.
  • 6–7 PM danger window — keep cut carrot + cucumber ready in the fridge. That's what your hand grabs, not the sweets box. Environment beats willpower.
🥕 The carrot test — when an urge hits, ask: "would I eat a bowl of raw carrot right now?"
Yes → you're really hungry. Eat it. Done, for 25 kcal.
No, I want the sweet → that's craving, not hunger. Food won't fix it — walk away, cocoa milk, brush your teeth.
  • Raw veg is unlimited — fruit is NOT. Fruit has real sugar; that's why the chart gives you one, at dinner.
  • No sugar or honey in the tea — a sweetened cup ≈ half a sweet.
  • No coconut milk / ghee-heavy soup — that's 250+ kcal. Not a soup, a small meal.
  • Specific yes, not a general one. Raw veg + thin savoury drinks only. Anything solid, sweet or fried is still off-plan — if you're thinking "extras are fine" about a vadai, that's the loophole talking.
  • Hungry (carrot-test-passing) every single afternoon? That's the plan under-fuelling you, not weak willpower. Tell me — we make lunch bigger. Don't paper over it.

🍬 Sweets — 1 a day, and that's fine

Not a ban. Bans are what produce the 4-sweet day. One a day means nothing is ever forbidden, so nothing ever needs a binge to get at.
  • One sweet a day. Best right after training. On a plate, sitting down — never from the box.
  • Trace amounts don't count — a sip of hot chocolate, half a bite. Count real indulgences: a whole sweet, a glass of something sugary.
  • The rest go out of sight — cupboard, opaque box, another room. You cannot out-willpower a sweet that's on the counter.
  • Visitors bring sweets? Portion and cupboard them within 5 minutes of arrival, before you've eaten one.
  • Craving hard after a big training day? That's fuel debt, not weakness — your body wants carbs. Eat more real food first (rice, dal, fruit), then decide about the sweet. This is the one that got you to 4 sweets in Week 2.

🥚 Protein — why the portions are what they are

Not a target to hit. Just the reason the sundal, dal, eggs and curd are sized the way they are — so you don't quietly shrink them.
  • Where you land: ~90 g a day. Country eggs are small (~4.5 g each, not 6.5), so the eggs give less than people assume — the sundal, dal, curd and milk do a lot of the work.
  • Why ~90 matters: you're lifting and in a deficit. Protein is the thing that stops your body taking the difference out of muscle instead of fat. Below ~90 g you still lose weight — you just hand back more muscle with it. That works against "leaner and muscular".
  • The portions doing the work: sundal = 1 full cup (not a small one) · dal = a generous bowl · 3 eggs at breakfast and 3 at dinner · a full cup of curd · a big glass of night milk.
  • These are the portions most likely to quietly shrink — they get served by someone else. If the sundal keeps arriving small, that's ~7 g gone every day and you'd never notice.
  • Still no gram-counting. You don't track this. It's built into the chart. Eat the portions and it takes care of itself.
Whey protein? Not needed. Whey is just dried milk protein — it does nothing a full cup of sundal doesn't. Only reconsider it if: the bigger portions aren't actually happening, or your lifts stall while eating and sleeping fine, or you're losing weight fast but looking soft. Any of those → one scoop in the night milk, done.

🥄 Ghee & fats — the invisible 150 kcal

Ghee is not bad for you — it's fine fat, it helps you absorb vitamins from the keerai/veg, and it makes food taste good (which is what keeps you on the plan). Don't cut it to zero. But it's calorie-dense and invisible — you don't chew it, you don't feel full from it, you don't remember eating it.
  • 1 teaspoon ghee~45 kcal
  • 1 tablespoon / serving spoon~120 kcal
  • "A spoon or two" of the big spoon~240 kcal — 12% of your day
Which 150 kcal do you want to give up? Cut rice → you lose fullness and you're hungry by 4 PM. Cut ghee → you feel literally nothing. Same plate, same food. Ghee is the free win — take it.
  • 1 level teaspoon, twice a day max (~2 tsp / ~90 kcal total).
  • Add it yourself, at your plate — not in the kitchen, not into the pot. Ghee in the pot is unmeasurable and unlimited; ghee you spoon onto your own plate is countable. Ask amma to keep it on the table, not in the cooking.
  • Paneer subji: normal tempering, not a generous one. Paneer brings its own fat.
Net: ~240 → ~90 kcal/day ≈ 0.5 kg of fat a month, for zero hunger and zero willpower. Nothing else here gives that ratio.

🍿 If you want a snack — the approved list

Pick ONE a day (that's the 4 PM slot). Not free like raw veg — these have real calories, they're just the cheapest way to satisfy a chewing habit.
  • Plain popcorn, home-popped — up to 3 big cups
    whole grain, mostly air, takes ages to eat — your best option    ~90 kcal
  • Roasted chana / peanuts — 1 cupped hand~120 kcal
The traps — these are the real ones for you:
  • Butter / caramel / cheese popcorn, or microwave packets — 150–200+ kcal per cup. That's a dessert, not a snack. Kadai-popped with a few drops of oil + salt is the only version that counts.
  • Pori urundai (jaggery pori balls) = a sweet. Counts against your one-a-day.
  • Both are bowl-diving foods — exactly your pattern. Portion into a bowl, put the packet away, then sit down. Never eat from the packet.
  • Plain pori (puffed rice) is a weak choice — refined, no fibre, ~55 kcal/cup, and you'll be hungrier an hour later. If you want it, make kara pori (onion, tomato, carrot, coriander, lemon) so it actually fills you.

🍎 Fruit — 1 a day, at dinner

No fixed fruit per day — eat whatever's at home. Only the amount is fixed, because fruit has real sugar. (Raw veg is unlimited; fruit is not.)
  • 1 fruit = 1 fist, or 1 cup if it's cut~60–90 kcal
  • Sweet/dense fruit = HALF that (½ cup)
    mango, jackfruit, chikoo, grapes, custard apple, banana, sitaphal    ~2× the sugar
  • Watery fruit = full portion, no worry watermelon, muskmelon, papaya, guava, orange, mosambi, apple
  • No juice. A glass of juice = 3–4 fruits with the fibre stripped out. Eat it, don't drink it.
  • 1 fruit a day, at dinner. The banana after your workout is separate — that one's fuel, not dessert.

☕ The cocoa milk — what it's actually for

It isn't a health drink. It's a substitution tool, placed exactly where you break.
  • 1 glass warm milk + 1 tsp plain cocoa, no sugar. ~110 kcal.
  • Hold it for the moment you want the sweets box. That's the job. If the urge comes at 9 PM, drink it at 9 PM.
  • ONE glass a day — one per day, not one per urge.
  • Plain bitter cocoa only. NOT Bournvita / Boost / Horlicks / drinking chocolate — those are ~70% sugar. One glass of that ≈ one sweet.
  • Train in the evening? This same glass is your post-workout milk. Don't add a second one.

🎉 Free meal (not a "cheat")

One planned free meal a week — Saturday lunch, earned after the long run. Eat whatever you want, zero guilt, then straight back to the chart at dinner.
  • Why not "cheat day"? "Cheat" implies you did something wrong. Guilt is what powers the restrict → binge → guilt → restrict loop. This is a planned part of the plan, not a failure.
  • Why Saturday, not Sunday? Sunday is your rest day and your danger day — home all day, bored, sweets in sight. Don't hand yourself a free pass on the day you're least defended.
  • One meal. Not one day. Dinner on Saturday is back on the chart.

🚫 Things we are NOT doing

Written down so they don't creep back in.
  • No gram-counting. No protein targets. Your normal food already gets you there. Chasing a number turns eating into a chore, and chores get abandoned.
  • No weighing scale. Hands and one fixed bowl. Precision was never the problem — habit was.
  • No banning sweets. One a day, forever.
  • No out-training the diet. Extra cardio to "earn" food doesn't work — it just makes you hungrier and wrecks your run recovery. The sweets dial does in 5 seconds what an hour of cardio can't.
  • No starving on training days. Under-eat + train hard = a forced binge. If you're genuinely hungry, eat more at lunch and tell me.
